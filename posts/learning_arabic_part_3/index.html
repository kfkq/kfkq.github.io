Most cases of plural

In most cases, making plural in Arabic, you simply add a و and ن to get plural from singular, for example, from مُجْرِمٌ to مُجْرِمُوْنَ. If it is feminine, a noun with ة, to make its plural, remove the ة and add an ا and a ت, for example from غُرْفَةٌ to غُرُفَاتٌ. Second consonant of the feminine noun may change its harakah depending on the kind of noun.

For other words, set patterns are to be followed by learning from hearing or from dictionaries. A single noun can appear in several plural forms. For example, كَافِرٌ to كَافِرُونَ, كُفَّارُ, كَفَرَةُ. Those plural that we just listed always end with “dammah”. This applies to all plurals that appear acquiring forms: أَفْعِلاَءُ, مَفَاعِلُ, فُعَلاَءُ and فَعِيلَةُ).

The rest of pattern (regular, broken plural, and irregular), please look the pattern table in textbook.
