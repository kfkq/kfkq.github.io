2024.06.18 · Learning Arabic (Plural) Part 3 · · #arabic, #notes,

Abstract : My notes and short explanation on Mudaf and Mudaf Ilayhi (Copied from Textbook) + Solution to Exercise. Textbook used in this notes: An Introduction to the Arabic Language Through Islamic Texts Vol I. Most cases of plural In most cases, making plural in Arabic, you simply add a و and ن to get plural from singular, for example, from مُجْرِمٌ to مُجْرِمُوْنَ. If it is feminine, a noun with ة, to make its plural, remove the ة and add an ا and a ت, for example from غُرْفَةٌ to غُرُفَاتٌ.
