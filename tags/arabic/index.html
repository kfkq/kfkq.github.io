Learning Arabic (Plural) Part 3

Abstract : My notes and short explanation on Mudaf and Mudaf Ilayhi (Copied from Textbook) + Solution to Exercise. Textbook used in this notes: An Introduction to the Arabic Language Through Islamic Texts Vol I. Most cases of plural In most cases, making plural in Arabic, you simply add a و and ن to get plural from singular, for example, from مُجْرِمٌ to مُجْرِمُوْنَ. If it is feminine, a noun with ة, to make its plural, remove the ة and add an ا and a ت, for example from غُرْفَةٌ to غُرُفَاتٌ.

Learning Arabic (Mudaf and Mudaf Ilayhi) Part 2

Abstract : My notes and short explanation on Mudaf and Mudaf Ilayhi (Copied from Textbook) + Solution to Exercise. Textbook used in this notes: An Introduction to the Arabic Language Through Islamic Texts Vol I. Construct and Genitive (الْمُضَافُ وَالْمُضَافُ أِلَيْهِ) In english you say “Name of the book”. In arabic the structure is the same way with few grammatical rules are involved. The construction is called as “mudaaf-mudaaf ilayhi”. The first noun ins called “mudaaf” (construct) and second, “mudaaf ilayhi” (Genitive).
